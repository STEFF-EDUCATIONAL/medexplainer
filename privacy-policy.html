Privacy Policy

Effective date: 19 February 2026
This Privacy Policy explains how MEDEXPLAINER (“we”, “us”, “Service”) handles information when you use MedExplainer. This policy is written for Phase 1–2: stateless AI analysis + local storage on device only.

Phase 1–2: Stateless + Local storage No account required No ads / no trackers
Important: MedExplainer provides informational explanations of user-provided medical information. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment, and does not replace consultation with a qualified physician.
Emergency notice: If you think you may have a medical emergency, contact local emergency services immediately.
1) Controller / Operator & Contact Who to contact

Data Controller / Operator: MEDEXPLAINER
Jurisdiction: Cyprus
Contact: support@medexplainer.online

2) Scope of this policy What it covers

This policy applies to the MedExplainer website and the MedExplainer mobile application (together, the “Service”). It does not cover third-party websites or services that may be linked from the Service.

3) Information you provide User-provided content
  • Text you enter (e.g., symptoms, questions, lab values you paste).
  • Files you upload (e.g., PDFs or images of medical documents, imaging results, photos you choose to provide).
  • Age you enter (required) to provide informational context (e.g., typical reference ranges vary by age). We do not use age to identify you.
  • Name (optional) if you choose to enter it. You can use the Service without providing your name.
You control what you submit. Please avoid uploading information you do not want processed for generating your informational report.
4) What we do NOT collect in Phase 1–2 Minimization
  • No account data. We do not require registration, and we do not maintain user profiles in Phase 1–2.
  • No server-side medical history. We do not store a longitudinal health diary on our servers in Phase 1–2.
  • No advertising identifiers. We do not use IDFA/advertising IDs for tracking.
  • No analytics SDKs for tracking across apps. We do not integrate third-party analytics or ad SDKs for cross-app tracking in Phase 1–2.
5) How the Service processes your information Stateless processing

When you submit a query or upload documents, the Service sends your input to an AI processing provider via API to generate an informational explanation. The Service is designed to be stateless: inputs are processed to produce the response and are not stored as a server-side user history in Phase 1–2.

  • Text input: processed to generate an explanation and a structured summary.
  • Images/PDFs: processed to extract text (including via OCR where needed) and generate an explanation.
  • Temporary files: if the Service creates temporary OCR files (e.g., PDF pages for processing), they are deleted after processing.
6) Local storage on your device On-device only

If you choose to save or export a report, it is stored on your device (for example, as a local file or within your device storage). In Phase 1–2 we do not provide server-side accounts and we do not store your saved reports on our servers.

7) Purposes of processing Why we process
  • Provide the Service by generating informational explanations and summaries from your inputs.
  • Safety and reliability (e.g., prevent abuse, maintain service integrity). In Phase 1–2 we do not log full medical text payloads for this purpose.
8) Sharing & third parties Who receives data

We share your submitted content only as needed to provide the Service (for example, with the AI API provider used to generate the explanation). We do not sell your personal information and we do not share it with advertisers in Phase 1–2.

If you use “Share” functionality, you choose the recipient (e.g., email, messaging apps). This sharing is initiated by you.
9) Data retention How long
  • No server-side history retention (Phase 1–2). The Service does not keep a server-side medical history tied to you.
  • Temporary processing files (if created for OCR) are deleted after processing.
  • Local files (saved reports) remain on your device until you delete them.
10) Security Protection measures

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect information during transmission and processing. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we aim to minimize risk, including by not storing server-side medical history in Phase 1–2.

11) Children Age considerations

The Service is intended for general informational use. If you are a parent or guardian and provide information about a minor, you are responsible for providing that information and supervising its use. If you believe a child has provided information without appropriate consent, contact us at support@medexplainer.online.

12) International processing Cross-border

AI processing providers and hosting infrastructure may be located in different countries. Where applicable, transfers are made to provide the Service.

13) Changes to this Privacy Policy Updates

We may update this policy from time to time. We will revise the “Effective date” at the top of this page when changes are made.

14) Contact Support

Questions about this Privacy Policy: support@medexplainer.online
